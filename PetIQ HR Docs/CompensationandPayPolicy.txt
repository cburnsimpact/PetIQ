PetIQ Compensation and Pay Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2025
Policy Number: HR-401

PURPOSE

This policy outlines PetIQ's compensation philosophy, pay practices, and guidelines for employee compensation. PetIQ is committed to providing fair, competitive, and equitable compensation to all employees.

COMPENSATION PHILOSOPHY

PetIQ's compensation program is designed to:

Attract and retain qualified talent
Reward performance and contributions
Remain competitive within our industry and geographic markets
Ensure internal equity and consistency
Comply with all federal, state, and local wage laws
Support our company values and business objectives
EMPLOYMENT CLASSIFICATION

Exempt Employees:

Salaried positions exempt from overtime under FLSA
Paid on a salary basis regardless of hours worked
Includes managerial, professional, and executive roles
Must meet FLSA salary threshold and duties test
Non-Exempt Employees:

Paid hourly and eligible for overtime pay
Must accurately record all hours worked
Entitled to meal and rest breaks per state law
Overtime paid at 1.5x regular rate for hours over 40 per week
Full-Time: 30+ hours per week, benefits eligible
Part-Time: 20-29 hours per week, limited benefits
Temporary: Fixed-term employment, no benefits unless required by law

PAY STRUCTURE

Salary Grades and Ranges:

PetIQ uses a structured salary grade system with defined minimum, midpoint, and maximum ranges for each position:

Grade 1 (Entry-Level): $35,000 - $45,000
Grade 2 (Associate): $45,000 - $58,000
Grade 3 (Professional): $58,000 - $75,000
Grade 4 (Senior Professional): $75,000 - $95,000
Grade 5 (Specialist/Team Lead): $85,000 - $110,000
Grade 6 (Manager): $95,000 - $130,000
Grade 7 (Senior Manager): $115,000 - $155,000
Grade 8 (Director): $140,000 - $190,000
Grade 9 (Senior Director): $170,000 - $230,000
Grade 10 (Vice President): $200,000 - $280,000
Executive: Individually determined

Hourly Wage Ranges:

Level 1 (Entry): $16.00 - $19.00/hour
Level 2 (Experienced): $19.00 - $24.00/hour
Level 3 (Senior/Specialized): $24.00 - $32.00/hour
Level 4 (Lead/Supervisor): $28.00 - $38.00/hour

Job Placement:

Positions are assigned grades based on responsibilities, required skills, and market data
HR conducts regular market reviews to ensure competitive positioning
Employees can request job reclassification through their manager if duties change significantly
PAY PERIODS AND PAYDAYS

Pay Schedule:

Employees are paid bi-weekly (every two weeks)
26 pay periods per year
Pay periods run Sunday through Saturday
Payday is Friday following the end of the pay period
Direct Deposit:

All employees must enroll in direct deposit
Paychecks deposited by 9:00 AM on payday
Set up through employee portal or provide voided check to HR
Can split deposit between multiple accounts
Pay Statements:

Available electronically through employee portal
Includes: gross pay, deductions, taxes, YTD totals, PTO balance
Review carefully and report any discrepancies to Payroll immediately
OVERTIME

Non-Exempt Employees:

Overtime paid at 1.5x regular hourly rate
Calculated for hours worked over 40 in a workweek
Overtime must be pre-approved by manager
Unauthorized overtime may result in disciplinary action
Overtime Calculation:

Only actual hours worked count toward overtime
PTO, sick time, and holidays do NOT count as hours worked
Travel time may be compensated per FLSA guidelines
Exempt Employees:

Not eligible for overtime pay
Expected to work hours necessary to complete job duties
Typically 40-45 hours per week; extended hours may be needed during peak periods
STARTING PAY

New Hire Compensation:
Starting salary/wage is determined by:

Position grade and salary range
Relevant experience and qualifications
Internal equity with current employees
Market conditions and budget constraints
Typically starts at minimum to midpoint of range
Negotiation:

PetIQ considers candidate requests within budgetary constraints
Final offers approved by HR and hiring manager
All offers contingent on background check and references
MERIT INCREASES AND PROMOTIONS

Annual Merit Reviews:

Conducted every January
Based on performance evaluation ratings
Effective first pay period in February
Budget allocated annually (typically 3-5% of payroll)
Merit Increase Guidelines:

Exceeds Expectations: 4-6% increase
Meets Expectations: 2-4% increase
Needs Improvement: 0-2% increase
Unsatisfactory: No increase
Performance Ratings:

Based on goal achievement, competencies, and behaviors
Rated by direct manager with input from peers/stakeholders
Discussed in annual review meeting
Must complete review to be eligible for increase
Promotions:

New salary typically increases 8-12% or to new grade minimum, whichever is higher
Must have been in current role at least 12 months (exceptions possible)
Promotion requires updated job description and HR approval
Effective date typically first of the month following approval
Equity Adjustments:

May be granted outside normal cycle for market competitiveness or internal equity
Requires business case and executive approval
Not tied to performance
BONUSES AND INCENTIVE COMPENSATION

Annual Bonus Program:

Eligible employees: Grades 5 and above
Target bonus: 10-20% of base salary depending on grade
Based on company performance and individual performance
Paid in March following performance year
Must be employed on payout date to receive
Sales Commission:

Sales roles have commission plans in addition to base salary
Commission structure detailed in individual Sales Compensation Plans
Paid monthly or quarterly depending on role
Subject to clawback for returns/cancellations
Spot Bonuses:

Discretionary recognition awards ($250-$2,500)
For exceptional contributions or project completion
Nominated by manager, approved by Director and HR
Paid on next regular paycheck
Taxed as supplemental income
Referral Bonuses:

$1,000 for successful referral of qualified candidate
$2,000 for hard-to-fill or specialized positions
Paid 50% after 90 days, 50% after 6 months of employment
Referrer must be employed at both payout dates
See Employee Referral Program policy for details
COMPENSATION CHANGES

Mid-Year Adjustments:
Compensation may change mid-year due to:

Promotion to new position
Significant change in job duties
Market competitiveness adjustments
Change in employment status (FT to PT or vice versa)
Transfer to different location with different pay rates
Demotion or Transfer:

Pay may be reduced if moving to lower-graded position
Typically 30-day notice given for pay reductions
Employee may choose to resign rather than accept reduction
PAY TRANSPARENCY

Discussing Pay:

PetIQ complies with all pay transparency laws
Employees have the right to discuss their own compensation
Retaliation for discussing wages is prohibited
Managers should not ask candidates about salary history where prohibited by law
Job Postings:

Salary ranges included in job postings where required by law
Ranges represent the full pay scale for the position
PAY EQUITY

Equal Pay:

PetIQ is committed to pay equity regardless of race, gender, age, or other protected characteristics
Regular audits conducted to identify and address pay disparities
Compensation decisions based on legitimate business factors
Salary History Ban:

PetIQ does not ask candidates about previous salary in states where prohibited
Compensation based on market data and internal equity
DEDUCTIONS FROM PAY

Standard Deductions:

Federal, state, and local income taxes
Social Security and Medicare (FICA)
Health insurance premiums
401(k) contributions
FSA contributions
Garnishments (child support, tax levies, etc.)
Voluntary Deductions:

Supplemental insurance (life, disability, etc.)
Parking fees
Employee purchases
Charitable contributions
Improper Deductions:
Exempt employees will not have salary deducted for:

Partial day absences (except first/last week of employment)
Jury duty or military leave
Absences due to company closure
Quality or quantity of work
If you believe an improper deduction was made, report immediately to HR.

TIMEKEEPING (Non-Exempt Employees)

Recording Time:

Accurately record all hours worked using time clock system
Clock in/out for shifts and meal breaks
Round to nearest 15 minutes
Review and approve timesheet before submission
Meal Breaks:

Unpaid 30-minute break for shifts over 5 hours
Must clock out for meal breaks
Cannot work during unpaid meal break
Rest Breaks:

Paid 10-minute breaks for every 4 hours worked
No need to clock out for rest breaks
Timesheet Approval:

Submit timesheet by Monday 9 AM following end of pay period
Manager reviews and approves
Late or inaccurate timesheets delay paycheck processing
Falsification:

Falsifying time records is grounds for immediate termination
Includes recording time for another employee
Working off the clock is prohibited
EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT

Business Expenses:

Employees reimbursed for reasonable, pre-approved business expenses
Submit through expense portal within 30 days
Requires itemized receipts over $25
Reimbursed on next regular paycheck
Mileage:

Current IRS standard mileage rate (67 cents/mile for 2025)
Personal vehicle use must be pre-approved
Calculate using mapping software (Google Maps, MapQuest)
No reimbursement for regular commute to office
FINAL PAYCHECK

Upon Termination:

Final paycheck includes all wages earned through last day worked
Accrued, unused PTO paid out (up to 80 hours) if employee provided 2 weeks notice
Deductions for unreturned equipment or outstanding balances
Paid on next scheduled payday or per state law requirements
PAYROLL CORRECTIONS

Reporting Errors:

Report any paycheck errors to payroll@petiq.com immediately
Include: pay period, error description, supporting documentation
Corrections made on next regular paycheck
Emergency checks issued for significant errors over $500
EMPLOYMENT VERIFICATION

Verification Requests:

All employment and income verifications must go through HR
Use The Work Number for automated verifications (1-800-367-5690, Company Code 12345)
Allow 3-5 business days for manual verifications
Manager may confirm dates of employment only, no salary information
CONFIDENTIALITY

Pay Information:

Individual compensation is confidential
Managers must not share employee pay information
Payroll staff bound by strict confidentiality
Unauthorized disclosure subject to disciplinary action
COMPLIANCE

Wage and Hour Laws:
PetIQ complies with:

Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)
State and local wage laws
Equal Pay Act
Pay transparency laws
Salary history ban laws
Questions about wage and hour compliance should be directed to HR immediately.
